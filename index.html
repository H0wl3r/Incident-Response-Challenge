Incident Response Challenge:
Uncovering the Root Cause

Scenario Overview

A user, "John Doe," has been researching ways to protect his family computer. However, his system soon begins exhibiting unusual behavior. Concerned that the system may be compromised or malfunctioning, John seeks expert assistance to investigate the issue, determine the root cause, and restore his computer to a secure and functional state.

Your Role

You are part of an incident response team tasked with identifying the root cause of the suspicious behavior. Your objectives include:

  1. Conducting a Root Cause Analysis
    • Investigate the system changes and network activity to uncover the source of the unusual behavior.
    • Identify whether the incident is the result of malware, misconfiguration, or other factors.
  2. Documenting Findings in a Detailed Incident Report
    • Record all observations, analyses, and conclusions in a structured and professional format.
  3. Recommending Remediation Actions
    • Suggest immediate and long-term actions to restore system integrity and prevent recurrence of similar incidents.

Guidance for Investigation

As you proceed, include the following in your analysis:

  • Tactics, Techniques, and Procedures (TTPs): Describe any adversarial methods identified during the investigation (e.g., phishing, privilege escalation, persistence mechanisms).
  • Tools Used: List and explain the purpose of tools or utilities used during the investigation (e.g., packet capture tools, forensic analysis software).
  • Commands Run: Detail specific commands executed and their purpose in gathering system or network data (e.g., netstat, ps, top).
  • Impact to the User or System: Assess and document the impact of the incident, such as data loss, system unavailability, or security risks.

Expected Deliverables

  • Root Cause Analysis Report:
    • Overview of the issue.
    • Detailed analysis of system and network activity.
    • Identified root cause with supporting evidence.
  • Recommendations for Remediation:
    • Step-by-step remediation plan.
    • Long-term recommendations for monitoring and prevention.
  • Incident Timeline:
    • Chronological account of events, from the user's observation of the issue to resolution.
